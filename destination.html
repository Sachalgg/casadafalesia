The Destination

Pipa

A small village perched above dramatic Atlantic cliffs in northeast Brazil — known for dolphins, surfers, bohemian culture, and one of the world's great sunsets.

The place

Why Pipa?

Pipa is one of those rare places that has somehow resisted the pull of mass tourism while becoming quietly beloved by those who know. It sits on the southern coast of Rio Grande do Norte, about 90km from Natal, where red clay cliffs drop straight into the Atlantic and a sheltered bay is home to a resident population of spinner dolphins.

The village itself is small, bohemian, and surprisingly refined — good restaurants, artisan shops, a handful of exceptional properties. But it is the landscape that keeps people returning: the cliffs, the beaches, the light.

The beaches

Beaches of Pipa

Praia da Pipa

The main beach, framed by cliffs and calm enough for swimming. The bay is where the dolphins congregate each morning.

Praia do Amor

A small, sheltered cove directly below the cliff — the most dramatic approach in Pipa, reached by a steep path.

Praia do Madeiro

The long, wilder beach to the south. Excellent surf and the quietest spot on the coast on weekday mornings.

Baía dos Golfinhos

The ecological reserve bay where spinner dolphins swim each morning — a remarkable and protected encounter.

When to come

Climate & best time

Northeast Brazil has one of the most reliable climates in the world. Pipa enjoys sun year-round, with the dry season running from September through February — the most popular period for good reason.

The rainy season (April–July) brings shorter showers and lush green cliffs, and the beaches are often emptier. The trade winds peak from August to December, making it prime kiteboarding season.

Getting here

How to reach Pipa

By Air

Fly to Natal — Aeroporto Internacional Governador Aluízio Alves (NAT).

  • São Paulo (GRU/CGH) — 3h direct (LATAM, Azul, Gol)
  • Rio de Janeiro (GIG/SDU) — 3h direct
  • Brasília — 2h30 direct
  • International via São Paulo or Fortaleza

Natal → Pipa

The airport is approximately 90km north of Pipa. Your options:

  • Private transfer (included with your stay) — 90 min, direct, comfortable
  • Pre-booked taxi service — approx. R$150–220
  • Shared van service — approx. 2h, R$40–60

International

Flying from outside Brazil? The most common routes:

  • Lisbon (LIS) → Natal — 7h30 direct (TAP)
  • Paris (CDG) → São Paulo → Natal
  • London (LHR) → São Paulo → Natal
  • Miami (MIA) → São Paulo → Natal
